Recommending Restaurants and Creating User Profiles

About


You know that friend who has such good taste in restaurants that you’ll go anywhere they recommend, no questions asked? Well guess what...there are plenty of those people out there and they’re already telling you all of the great places to go. And it just so happens we can harness them with the power of collaborative filtering.

In the age of AI, are we really still searching for restaurants based on simple attribute filtering and (I almost can’t even say it)...mean star rating? We deserve better than having to sort through countless images and reviews to figure out if an eatery suits our tastes. And restaurants who happen to be making their target market happy (even if it’s “subpar” by many people’s standards) deserves better than to be pegged as having a low rating.

Big Picture


Utilizing recommenders for restaurants could unlock a whole new level of information for consumers. All stakeholders win in this scenario. The customers get recommendations tailored to them, reducing the time needed for research and finding new locations they might otherwise miss. Restaurants aren't penalized for doing what they do best with a low rating, but if their piece of the market isn't quite as large as others, they can make changes as they see fit. Increased customer engagement will drive more dining and therefore greater revenue for restaurants. And the recommending company constantly builds its dataset from which it can derive value, specifically a revenue stream for optimizing marketing for restaurants based on user profiles.
